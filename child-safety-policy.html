Zajedno: Child Safety & Protection Policy

Last Updated: October 11, 2025

1. Our Unwavering Commitment to Child Safety

At Zajedno (Balkanski Muslim Dating), our mission is to provide a safe, Halal-compliant, and culturally sensitive platform for Balkan Muslims to find a marital partner. The safety and well-being of our community, and the broader online community, are of paramount importance.

We have a zero-tolerance policy towards any form of Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) and Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM). We unequivocally condemn and strictly prohibit any activity on our platform that involves, promotes, or facilitates the abuse or exploitation of children. This policy outlines the standards and measures we have in place to protect children and maintain a safe environment for all our users.

2. Definitions

Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE): Any act or activity involving the sexual abuse or exploitation of a minor. This includes, but is not limited to, grooming, coercion, and any sexual communication with a minor.

Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM): Any visual depiction (such as a photograph, video, or digital image) that shows a minor engaged in sexually explicit conduct.

3. Prohibited Conduct

The following activities are strictly forbidden on the Zajedno platform and will result in immediate and permanent termination of the user's account:

  • Creating, uploading, sharing, or soliciting CSAM.
  • Engaging in any conversation or behavior aimed at grooming or sexually exploiting a minor.
  • Impersonating a minor or attempting to contact individuals believed to be minors for sexual purposes.
  • Sharing links to external websites or platforms containing CSAE or CSAM.
  • Any other behavior that violates child safety laws or puts a minor at risk.

4. Our Safety Measures: Prevention and Detection

We employ a multi-layered approach to prevent and detect CSAE on our platform:

  • Proactive Content Moderation: We utilize advanced automated systems, including technologies like Amazon Rekognition, to proactively scan user-uploaded content (such as profile photos) for nudity and other policy-violating material. This helps us detect and remove inappropriate content before it becomes widely visible.
  • In-App User Reporting Tools: We empower our users to be our partners in safety. Our application provides clear and easily accessible tools for users to report any profile, message, or content they believe to be suspicious, inappropriate, or in violation of our child safety standards. Every report is reviewed by our safety team.
  • Account Verification: We have implemented account verification measures to help ensure the authenticity of profiles on our platform, adding a layer of security and trust.
  • Account Audits: We conduct regular reviews of accounts that have been flagged for suspicious activity.

5. Reporting Process and Enforcement

How to Report: Users can report a safety concern directly through a user's profile within the app. We encourage our users to report any suspicious behavior immediately.

Our Review Process: Upon receiving a report, our dedicated safety team will conduct a thorough review of the reported content and the user's activity. We treat all reports related to child safety with the highest priority.

Consequences: Any user found to be in violation of this Child Safety Policy will be immediately and permanently banned from the Zajedno platform. We do not offer second chances for such offenses.

6. Cooperation with Law Enforcement

Zajedno is fully committed to cooperating with national and international law enforcement agencies to combat CSAE.

  • We will report all credible instances of CSAM to the relevant national authorities in accordance with applicable laws (e.g., local and regional police cybercrime units).
  • Where applicable, we will also report CSAM to organizations such as the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).
  • We will preserve evidence related to CSAE and provide it to law enforcement upon receipt of a valid legal process.

7. Resources for Help

If you or someone you know is a victim of child abuse or exploitation, help is available. Please contact your local authorities immediately. You can also find support from the following organizations:

  • INHOPE: International Association of Internet Hotlines - www.inhope.org
  • Brave Phone (Hrabri Telefon), Croatia: www.hrabritelefon.hr
  • Plavi Telefon, Bosnia and Herzegovina: www.plavitelefon.ba
  • Your local police department.
