Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 11, 2026

RelayHome is a shared household coordination app for handoffs, routines, timers, queues, focus mode, subjects, and subscriptions. This Privacy Policy explains what we collect, why we collect it, who processes it, and how users can request export or deletion.

1. Data RelayHome collects

  • Account identifiers: email address, Supabase user ID, and Apple or Google sign-in data when social sign-in is used.
  • Display/profile data: display name and optional Apple-provided first and last name.
  • Household data: household name, memberships, roles, invite metadata, and permissions.
  • Task and relay content: task titles, notes, boomerang reasons, timelines, workflow templates, timers, and queue state.
  • Subject data: names and types for children, pets, rooms, appliances, deliveries, vehicles, and optional routine notes.
  • Device and push data: device ID, platform, push token reference, and notification delivery metadata.
  • Subscription data: RevenueCat app user ID, entitlement, product ID, purchase state, and restore state.
  • Diagnostics: crash logs, app version, build number, and device/runtime metadata.

2. What RelayHome does not collect in the MVP

  • No contacts permission.
  • No location permission.
  • No photos, videos, audio, or files permission.
  • No calendar permission.
  • No health or medical data.
  • No advertising ID.
  • No ads and no third-party tracking.

3. How we use data

  • To create and authenticate accounts.
  • To create, join, and manage households.
  • To run household relays, timers, queues, focus mode, and templates.
  • To deliver push notifications and handoff reminders.
  • To process subscriptions and restore entitlements.
  • To diagnose crashes, protect service integrity, and improve reliability.
  • To respond to support, privacy, export, and deletion requests.

4. Third-party processors

  • Supabase: authentication, database, edge functions, and realtime.
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging and Apple Push Notification service: push delivery.
  • Firebase Crashlytics: crash diagnostics.
  • RevenueCat: subscriptions and entitlements.
  • Apple App Store and Google Play: purchases and subscription billing.
  • Apple and Google identity providers: social sign-in.

5. Household privacy

Household records are intended for active household members only. Sensitive routine notes should be visible only to permitted members. Users should not enter emergency, medical, legal, or regulated-care instructions as their only source of truth.

6. Data retention

Active household data is retained while the account or household is active. Free history may be limited by plan. Command, event, audit, and subscription records may be retained for security, integrity, fraud prevention, legal, tax, billing, or dispute reasons where required.

7. User rights and controls

  • Users can request household data export in the app.
  • Users can request account deletion in the app.
  • Users can request account deletion on the web at /relay-home/account-deletion.html.
